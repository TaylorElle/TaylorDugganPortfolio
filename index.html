Hi. I'm Taylor Duggan!

After getting my Bachelor's degree in Psychology and spending over 8 years in the non-profit and humanitarian aid sector, I knew it was time to go after a new challenge.

I took advantage of COVID-caused unemployment and went after a long-time dream - to become a software engineer. Through Thinkful and other avenues, I have been learning Full Stack Web Development and building my portfolio.

About me

I am ecstatic at the avenues in which Developers can create products and solve problems. I am most excited for the innovations that can be made for people around the world. There's so much to learn and it's an honor to be part of a team to make this happen.

When I'm not coding, I spend my time gardening, tackling HIIT workouts, running, and painting functional art. Even my hobbies show my love for creating and developing things to be the best they can be.

Portfolio

Alarm Clock App

This is an Alarm Clock App I built using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Forcing the user to complete different actions (i.e. math problems, popping bubbles, etc) each day to turn the alarm off, it is for user's that need extra assistance waking up. I learned to use different APIs with this App to accomplish the goal of having different problems each day.

Calendar App

This is a Calendar App I built. Using React, this allows users to share their calendar with their closest friends. For users that travel often, friends can share their itinerary with each other and make plans for when they're in the same location. I learned how to link this App with Google Maps.

Candle Company Website

This is a website I built for a candle company. Using Bootstrap, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, this candle company can display and sell their wares to users in the USA. I learned how to create a secure shopping experience for guest and returning users.

Waitlist App

I created an app for a Chiropractic office that does not make appointments. Users can join a waitlist that will tell them when the doctor is ready to see them. I learned how to implement to user types for this application (i.e. doctor and patient) to allow for different commands by each.
